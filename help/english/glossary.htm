  • AC Utility Power

    The power supplied from a standard wall outlet.

  • Automatic Voltage Regulation (AVR)

    The UPS will accord defined voltage range to transfer input voltage to adaptive voltage for output when input voltage out of defined range.

  • Boost

    The internal regulator of the UPS raises the voltage when the AC utility power drops below a defined point.

  • Buck

    The internal regulator of the UPS lowers the voltage when the AC utility power rises above a defined point.

  • Capacity

    The current level of battery charge.

  • ECO

    It's abbreviation of Economy.

  • Hibernation

    The computer will save data and turn off your monitor and hard disk. When you "wake" it from this mode, all open files and running programs are restored.

  • Lost communication, Loss of communication

    PowerPanel ® Personal is unable to monitor the UPS.

  • Nominal Voltage

    It's operating voltage of the battery backup to fit real voltage circumstance.

  • Notification

    A message informing the user power event has occurred.

  • N.R., Nerver Restart

    The UPS will not auto restart if the N.R option is selected.

  • On line

    Electricity is being supplied by the standard wall outlet.

  • Overload

    Exceeding the rated capability of the UPS by plugging in too many devices or too large of equipment. See also UPS Overloaded.

  • Power failure, Power lost

    AC utility power interruption such as a blackout.

  • Runtime

    The length of time the UPS supplies battery power for.

  • Schedule

    PowerPanel ® Personal can automatically start up and shut down a connected computer and equipment.

  • Voltage Sensitivity

    A function allows users to select the sensitivity mode according to the power quality and the equipment.
