This feature shows current AC voltage and the normal voltage rating of your UPS. This function helps your equipment not experience dangerous voltage levels. If the utility power or output voltage* is over or less than the voltage range you set, the UPS will switch to battery mode automatically. The narrow output voltage range and it could cause much battery loss; the wide output voltage range and it could cause your equipment damage.

There have High/Low Voltage Transfer and Nominal Voltage options to setup high/low utility voltage or operating voltage of the battery backup. You can choose the options as below:

  • High/Low Voltage Transfer

    Sets the high/low utility voltage threshold. The battery backup will attempt to improve the utility voltage when this threshold is exceeded.

  • Nominal Voltage

    Determine the operating voltage of the battery backup. The different voltages will affect high/low voltage threshold.

Note : This is not available for all UPS models.
*Note : The reference of utility power or output voltage is depends on UPS models.
